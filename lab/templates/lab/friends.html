Feeding 9 Billion

Feeding Nine Billion Challenge is a way to spark innovative and sustainable ideas to help combat food insecurity across the globe. Held every fall semester at the University of Guelph, this challenge helps bring together a multitude of creators and entrepreneurs in hopes to bring about lasting and worthwhile change to our community and the world.

Black Box Initiative

Food waste is currently not separately managed by the University of Guelph due to costly tipping fees. Through the use of pyrolysis and biochar, the Black Box Initiative hopes to make in-house organic waste management possible on the University of Guelph campus. Pyrolysis will reduce the total volume of organic waste, while creating valuable by-products for the agriculture and energy industries (biochar and biofuel). In order to integrate a sustainable waste management solution on campus using biochar, we aim to launch an initial small-scale unit to test the method’s effectiveness in addressing the volume reduction of organic waste, as well as the agricultural benefits of biochar created from food waste specifically, before recommending the university to invest in a large scale pyrolysis unit.

Coffe Cup Warriors

Each day, 25,000 coffees are purchased on-campus at the University of Guelph; the majority of these are sold in disposable, single-use coffee cups. Not only do these non-recyclable coffee cups create significant and unnecessary landfill waste, but they also require enormous natural resource inputs for manufacturing and transportation to the point-of-sale. Drawing on the past successes of behavioural change campaigns and studies (e.g. reduced public acceptance of smoking and disposable shopping bag use), we aim to reduce the use of disposable coffee cups on-campus, in favour of increased reusable mug use. We are designing educational adverts inspired by effective behavioural-change campaigns to be displayed around campus to encourage students to change their coffee cup purchasing habits. We want you to take initiative and decrease your use of single-use products in everyday life!

Food CampUS

Food CampUS movement is working to create a Food Security and Community Education certificate program at the University of Guelph. The curriculum will follow themes of food security, sustainability, agriculture and nutrition. University co-op students will share their knowledge about food systems with elementary school students.
